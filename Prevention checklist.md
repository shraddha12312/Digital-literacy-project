Task 5- b) Prevention checklist

1.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Secure your college email, GitHub, and social media accounts using app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) rather than just SMS.

2.Verify "Digital Arrest" Threats: If you receive a video call from someone claiming to be a CBI or Police officer threatening "digital arrest," hang up immediately. Real law enforcement agencies never conduct arrests or investigations via Skype or WhatsApp.

3.Audit App Permissions: Regularly check your smartphone settings and revoke "Contacts," "Camera," or "Gallery" access for apps that don't strictly need them.

4.Use Official App Stores Only: Avoid "modded" versions of apps (like WhatsApp Gold or cracked Spotify) from third-party sites, as these often contain spyware targeting Indian users.

5.Check for HTTPS: Before entering any login credentials on a library or public computer, ensure the URL starts with https:// and shows a padlock icon.

6.Secure Public Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing your bank or student portal while on free public Wi-Fi (like at railway stations or cafes) unless you are using a trusted VPN.

7.UPI Safety - No PIN for Receiving: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a "Collect Request" and asks for your PIN, it is a scam.

8.UPI Safety - Verify Merchant Names: Always double-check the actual name displayed on your UPI app after scanning a QR code at local shops or on stickers before hitting "Pay."
